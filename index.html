Protecting Ghana's Water from Illegal Mining (Galamsey)

An educational campaign on the urgent threat of water scarcity, siltation, and mercury contamination.

EXPLORE THE THREAT

What is Galamsey?
The Anatomy of an Illegal Operation

Galamsey is the Ghanaian term for illegal, unregulated small-scale gold mining. Unlike licensed mining, Galamsey operates without environmental safeguards or government oversight, directly undermining the nation's health and water security.

Types of Illegal Mining

  • Shallow Alluvial Mining: The popular 'dig and wash' method for deposits under 3 meters deep.
  • Deep Alluvial Mining: Excavating pits (7 to 12 meters deep) along major river banks (e.g., Pra, Ankobra).
  • Hard Rock (Lode) Mining: Mining gold-bearing reefs, sometimes illegally using prohibited explosives.

Tools and Equipment

Illegal operations range from rudimentary tools to heavy machinery:

  • Artisanal Tools: Shovels, pick-axes, pans, and sluice boxes.
  • Mechanized Tools: Water pumps, generators, excavators, bulldozers, and local chinvan machines.
  • Chemicals: Primarily Mercury (Hg) is the main chemical used for cheap and efficient gold extraction.

Persons Involved & Causes

Miners are often unemployed youth driven by poverty. Financiers (sometimes foreign) profit from the lack of regulation. This is driven by high unemployment, weak governance, and high global gold prices.

Areas Affected: Major rivers like the Pra, Tano, Birim, Ankobra, and Offin, which are vital drinking water sources.

The Invisible Toxin: Mercury's Scientific Journey

Galamsey introduces the highly dangerous neurotoxin, Mercury (Hg), into our water systems. This is how the contamination spreads and affects us. Drag the slider to see the progression of the invisible threat.

The Process: Bioaccumulation

Mercury is converted by bacteria into Methylmercury, which is then absorbed by small aquatic organisms. As larger fish eat smaller contaminated fish, the toxin level bio-magnifies dramatically, reaching toxic levels in fish, which are then consumed by humans.

Quantifying the Destruction: The True Cost

Environmental Effects

Siltation (Turbidity): Massive sediment runoff chokes rivers, blocking sunlight and destroying ecosystems. Contamination: High levels of Mercury, Arsenic, and Cadmium pollute the water and soil (Source 4.1, 6.1). Land Degradation: Widespread deforestation and pit digging.

Health & Social Effects

Health: Exposure to methylmercury causes severe, irreversible neurological damage, kidney malfunction, and birth defects. Miners face high risks of respiratory issues. Social: Leads to conflict, loss of traditional elihoods (fishing/farming), and increased water scarcity.

Economic Effects

The government loses over $500 million annually in unpaid taxes. Cleanup costs, environmental remediation, and the burden on the public health system create a massive economic strain (Source 8.1).

The Path to Clean Water: Policy, Prevention, and Proper Practice

Government Policies and Laws

The Minerals and Mining Act, 2006 (Act 703) is the core law, but enforcement remains the biggest challenge. Taskforces like *Operation Vanguard* attempt to clamp down on illegal activity.

Solutions & Preventive Measures

Promoting mercury-free mining techniques (like Gravity Concentration or Borax), rigorous border controls on chemicals, and high-level training for miners on formal procedures and health risks.
